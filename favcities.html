Favourite Cities

Hyderabad

Hyderabad is Telangana's largest and most populous city, and also it's capital. Hyderabad is the fourth-most populous city in India with a population of 6.9 million residents within the city limits, and has a population of 9.7 million residents in the metropolitan region, making it the sixth-most populous metropolitan area in India. Hyderabad was founded by the sultans of Golconda in the 16th century, the town was destroyed following the Mughal occupation in 1685. In 1724 it became the capital of the independent kingdom of Hyderabad.

Good Places To Visit In Hyderabad:

  • Charminar
  • Golconda Fort
  • Ramoji Film City
  • Mecca Masjid
  • Qutb Shahi Tombs


Mumbai

Mumbai, also known as Bombay, is the capital of Maharashtra. It is also the most populous city of India with an estimated city proper population of 12.5 million (1.25 crore) people. Mumbai is the financial, commercial, and the entertainment capital of India. Upon India's independence in 1947 the city was incorporated into Bombay State. In 1960, following the Samyukta Maharashtra Movement, a new state of Maharashtra was created with Mumbai as the capital.

Good Places To Visit In Mumbai:

  • Gateway Of India
  • The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus
  • Elephanta Caves
  • Sanjay Gandhi National Park


Kolkota

Kolkata is known as 'The City Of Joy' because people there like to and live a happy life. Kolkata's cusine includes a lot of delicious food like 'Freshwater fish curry' and for dessert they love Rasgulla. Kolkata is the seventh most populous city of India with an estimated city proper population of 4.5 million (0.45 crore). Kolkata is the second largest Bengali-speaking city in the world. Kolkata's architecture includes many imperial landmarks, including the Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge and the Grand Hotel.

Good Places To Visit In Kolkata:

  • Victoria Memorial
  • Howrah Bridge
  • Grand Hotel
  • Eden Gardens
  • Birla Mandir
